Pathfinders Robotics

Goals:

  1. Capability of processing payments from sponsors and parents
  2. Attract more sponsors interested in learning more about the organization and make donations to our nonprofit
  3. Place for sponsors and professionals to have direct contact to the organization and individual teams
  4. Have consistency across the website

Target Users:

  • Parents and children interested in creating/joining a team under our nonprofit
  • Sponsors
  • Existing Teams
  • Professionals

    Role:

    About the Foundation

    Field Studies / User Interviews

    My initial discovery phase involved learning about the purpose and initiatives of the organization. I collaborated with the board members of the organization and came up with this list of initiatives:

    • Receive payments from family members for season registration and fees
    • Receive sponsorships and donations from sponsors and donators
    • Place for prospective team members to learn about teams
    • Contact area for prospective team members to contact teams
    • Links to the FIRST robotics organization and it's programs
    • Showing the organization's community impact
    • Description of the nonprofit organization's charitable partnership and collaboration efforts

    Explore

    Card Sorting

    Using the list of website features I got from the field study, I did a variation of a card sorting excercise to sort each of the features of the website into different groups. This was done to finalize the information architecture of the website.

    Sitemap

    After sorting the featuers into groups, I decided to that these groups would be a good organization for the website, with each group being a separate page on the website.

    We used the colors from the logo as the colors for the website. Fonts were imported from the Blocksy website template and were kept because they resembled the font of the nonprofit branding.

    Prototype

    We wanted the homepage to serve as an introduction to the organization by showing the community initiatives and impact of the organization to attract and relate to our target users. We used motion design to direct the user's motion throughout the webpage. We also used hover effects to emphasize images and icons by increasing their size when hovered over.

    The about page was intended for the parents and potential team members. During our outreach efforts, we found that parents and children we talked to had a hard time navigating the FIRST organization website and didn't know the different programs offered in FIRST and which ones they could be involved in.

    The teams page was created as a way for potential team members and parents to learn about the teams and for sponsors to learn more about each of the teams within the organization. We originally intended to create a whole webpage for each team but with some teams being new and other teams changing we decided to softlaunch these as a section of a broader page and slowly create separate pages for individual teams. Some established, senior teams have existing websites that they have just routed to this page so users can easily navigate to their website for more information. After conversing with parents and sponsors, we will be releasing more features such as calendars and event notifications that will provide more information of these teams progress throughout the season to sponsors, parents, and other interested parties.

    The donate page is the most important page for the website. We wanted to ensure that sponsors and parents making payments had a very simple payment process. For this reason we included buttons of preset payments in the header of the page, that way people visiting the website to send us a payment would easily be able to navigate to the page and submit their payment. Underneath that we wanted to include our sponsorship tiers and information for better transparency. The source of this information came from our nonprofit's sponsorship pamphlet we sent to companies to gain sponsorships. We also included logistical information regarding the nonprofits initiatives.

    Our goal with the contact page was to make it direct and straightforward. Therefore we only include to sections to this page which are placed right next to each other. We included our contact information with helpful information for those who would like to reach us or send us materials, which we found extremely helpful when receiving teams' robotics tools and kits along with securly receiving checks from sponsor's who would deliver donations in person. To the right we included a direct contact form for people to directly message us through the website using the form. This form directly sends an email to all members of the team and nonprofit organization and has led to quick response times.
